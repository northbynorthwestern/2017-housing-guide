The Housing Guide

Foster-Walker Complex

1927 Orrington Avenue

Foster-Walker (usually called PLEX), is located right in the middle of campus and is the only all-single residence hall on campus. Men and women live in the gender-clustered 582 single rooms, with one bathroom for every four rooms. The first floor of the building has two dining halls and a c-store, and the package centre for South Campus is in the basement.

What residents say

“If you start with not knowing anyone in your suite, it will stay that way for the whole year. But it's okay because at this point, you have friends and you don't need to feel like you have to socialize with people in your suite. Plex has a dining room, mail room, and laundry, so it’s in a prime location for sure.”

Dana Choi, Weinberg Sophomore:

“It’s in such a great location, they’re all single rooms so you have some privacy, but they also have thin walls so you can hear people across the wall talking on the phone. It’s also a plus that the dining hall, convenience store and mail center is at the same place.”

Lizzy Ju, Medill Sophomore

“It’s fun if you live with friends, and having a single is great. How social it is really depends — it’s fun for me because I live with friends and know a lot of people that live there.”

Shiv Subrahmanian, McCormick Sophomore:

What non-residents say

““I know it’s all singles, and I’ve heard it isn’t very social at all, even though I’ve never been in a room before. It seems to be viewed as the last resort for sophomores.””

Jack Pieterse, McCormick freshman

““The location is five stars - it has a gym in front, a library to the side, a package center right below, and two dining halls right down””

Mark Jung, McCormick Freshman

““Plex is a dorm that seems pretty old and run down. It's nice, however, that a lot of sophomores live there, so I can see a good number of my friends when I visit.””

April Chien, Communication sophomore

““Plex seems to be an overall decent dorm. But socially, it seems pretty dead. I’ve heard a lot of opinions of it being completely non-social, and if you’re not in a suite with people you’re close with, it can be not a fun time.””

Upasana Pathak, McCormick freshman
